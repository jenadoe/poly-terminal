How to read this board

These labels describe whether a Polymarket price is structurally clean enough to reuse as a market-sentiment reference. Ready is intentionally rare: it means the price can stand on its own as a clean reference, not that the odds are correct or predictive.

Most non-Ready markets are not bad or unusable. They are flagged because the price may depend on a specific option, candidate, team, bucket, timing window, or resolution rule that can disappear when the market title is quoted alone. Context Required means the reference may still be useful when that missing context travels with it.

Methodology note

What the labels mean

Strata evaluates whether a market price can be used as a crowd-sentiment reference based on public market structure, wording, resolution sensitivity, option context, and timing risk.

  • Ready: clean market-sentiment reference with standard source attribution.
  • Context Required: attach the named option, wording, timing, or resolution context before reusing.
  • Review Recommended: potentially useful, but review wording, resolution source, timing, or sensitivity before reuse.
  • Not Standalone: do not use the price as an isolated market-sentiment reference.
Boundary note

What this does not mean

A Strata label is not a forecast, endorsement, odds validation, or investment recommendation. It only describes whether the market price can be reused as a market-sentiment reference without losing important structure or context.

  • No raw trades, wallets, or participant identifiers are shown.
  • No numeric rating is used as a public-facing claim.
  • No label should be interpreted as trading or outcome guidance.
