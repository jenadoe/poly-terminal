What Is Strata

Strata is a public market-confidence layer for prediction market prices. It is designed to help users read whether a market looks structurally reliable, still forming, or fragile under current market conditions.

The public dashboard is built to support interpretation. It gives a limited, read-only view of market-confidence signals and public-facing states so that users can understand the surface at a glance.

Strata does not predict outcomes, guarantee correctness, or provide trading advice. It is a public interpretation layer for market structure.

How to Read the Dashboard

The dashboard is meant to answer a simple question: how much structural trust should be placed in a market price right now?

Converged The public read suggests a price signal that looks comparatively stable.
Calibrating The market still appears to be forming or repricing.
Fragile The public read suggests caution when interpreting the current price.

Structural Snapshot

  • Markets tracked shows the number of markets currently represented on the public surface.
  • Active snapshots summarizes recent public snapshot activity across the surface.
  • Avg active depth gives a high-level sense of recent public context across tracked markets.
  • Judgments logged shows the number of public judgments visible on the surface.
These labels are designed to help with interpretation. They should not be read as guarantees, predictions, or investment recommendations.

Public Data Boundary

Strata's public surface is intentionally limited. It focuses on selected summaries, public-facing labels, and product interpretation.

  • The public site provides a read-only surface.
  • The public site is designed to communicate market-confidence context in a concise public format.
  • The public site should be read as a product surface, not as a technical specification.

This boundary keeps the public product understandable and focused on interpretation.

FAQ

Does Strata predict outcomes?

No. Strata is designed to help interpret market confidence, not to predict whether an outcome will happen.

Is this trading advice?

No. The public surface is informational and interpretive only.

Does the dashboard show everything Strata tracks?

No. The dashboard is a limited public view intended for public interpretation.

Should the public dashboard be treated as a final proof of market truth?

No. The public surface is meant to help users read market structure, not to certify any market as guaranteed truth.
