Methodology note

What the labels mean

Strata evaluates how a market price should be handled as a Polymarket reference based on public market structure, wording, resolution sensitivity, option context, timing risk, and whether the price may be contaminated by confirmation or oracle mechanics rather than clean public belief.

  • Ready - Cite with source: use the price as a Polymarket reference with a link and timestamp/source context.
  • Context Required - Attach context: keep the specific option, wording, timing, or resolution detail beside the price.
  • Review Recommended - Check belief contamination: inspect whether rules, confirmation timing, resolution criteria, oracle mechanics, or sensitivity are shaping the price before reuse.
  • Not Standalone - Do not quote alone: use only with full market wording or surrounding explanation.

Example: "2026 NBA Champion at 36%" loses the priced team. "Polymarket prices New York Knicks to win the 2026 NBA Finals at 36%" keeps the selected option attached, so the reference is much easier to reuse responsibly.

Boundary note

What this does not mean

A Strata label is not a forecast, endorsement, odds validation, or investment recommendation. It only describes whether the market price can be reused as a Polymarket reference without losing important structure or context.

  • No raw trades, wallets, or participant identifiers are shown.
  • No numeric rating is used as a public-facing claim.
  • No label should be interpreted as trading or outcome guidance.
Feedback ask

Three things we want reviewers to judge

We are testing whether this format fits real writing, research, dashboard, and AI-output workflows. Useful feedback is tied to a sentence you would publish or withhold.

  1. If you were writing about this market now, would you cite the price standalone?
  2. Did the status match your handling, over-warn, under-warn, or miss context?
  3. Was the suggested reference text publishable, too cautious, or still missing context?
